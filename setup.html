Computer Setup

All students must bring their own laptop running Windows 10. Your laptop should have a minimum of 4 GB of RAM and 128 GB of hard drive space. Those are minimum specs, and I recommend getting 8 GB of RAM and a 256 GB hard drive, but that’s your choice to make. If you already own a laptop and it’s older than that, send me an email and we can talk.

Your laptop must be running Windows 10.

As soon as possible after receiving your laptop, run through the following steps. If you’ve done a lot of your own configuration, some of these steps may have to change. If you run into ANY PROBLEMS, send me an e-mail at:

joel@theironyard.com

Again, we want to have all of these kinks worked out in advance, so PLEASE e-mail if things don’t work as described below.

  • Install Windows 10, if you haven’t already.
    1. If your laptop doesn't already have Windows 10 installed, you may have to upgrade. An upgrade can be purchased from any major retailer of office supplies or software.
    2. You can also purchase a downloadable upgrade here
    3. If you require use of any assistive technology - Microsoft grants you a free upgrade to Windows 10. Click here to get started.

    WARNING: The Windows 10 can take a bit of time to complete and will require a restart. Plan on doing this in the evening or over a lunch break.

  • Install Visual Studio
    1. Download Visual Studio Community 2015 from the Visual Studio website.
    2. Run the installer and follow the instructions to install.
    3. It can take a while to install - so clear your schedule.

  • Install Google Chrome

That should get you ready to start your first few days of class!
